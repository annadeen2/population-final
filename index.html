Will COVID-19 impact NYC population growth?

By Anna Deen, Nov. 6, 2020

Over the summer, Jerry Seinfeld famously shot back at “some putz on LinkedIn” by arguing in a New York Times op-ed column that NYC “isn’t dead.”

While NYC may not meet its population projections, it’s not necessarily because of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Demography, the science of populations, revolves around four factors: births, deaths, people moving in, and people moving out. According to a report by the Department of City Planning, more people have historically moved out of New York City than into it.

John Mollenkopf, a Distinguished Professor of Political Science and Sociology at the CUNY Graduate Center, says international migration into the city has largely prevented a drastic reduction in population. In part, he says the Trump administration’s attacks on immigration policy will cause the city to fall short of previous growth projections.

Mollenkopf says that while we don’t know if the COVID-19 pandemic will have long-term effects on New York City’s population, his uncertainty doesn’t keep him from being optimistic: “A lot of people think it’s transitory, and the city will come back. I’m sort of in that camp.”

Seinfeld will be relieved to hear it.
